Privacy Policy

This Privacy Notice for S&Z TRADING INTERNATIONAL S.C.A. ("we", "us", or "our") explains how and why we collect, store, use, and share your personal information when you use our services, including when you:

  • visit our website at https://szitrans.eu or any website of ours that links to this Privacy Notice; or

If you still have any questions or concerns, you can contact us at privacy@szitrans.com.

Summary of key points

This summary provides key points from our Privacy Notice. You can find out more about any of these topics in the sections that follow.

  • What personal information do we process? We process personal information that you provide to us, such as names, contact details, and shipment information, as well as certain technical information when you use our website (for example IP address, device and usage data).
  • Do we process sensitive information? We do not intentionally process special or sensitive categories of data (such as health data, religious beliefs, or similar). If such data is ever provided, we aim to delete it where possible.
  • Do we collect information from third parties? We do not intentionally obtain personal information about you from unrelated third-party data brokers.
  • How do we process your information? We process your information to provide and improve our services, communicate with you, ensure security and fraud prevention, comply with our legal obligations, and otherwise with your consent where required.
  • When and with whom do we share information? We may share your data with logistics partners, service providers, and authorities where necessary to provide our services or comply with law.
  • How do we keep your information safe? We use appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information. However, no electronic transmission or storage system can be guaranteed to be 100% secure.
  • What are your rights? Depending on where you are located, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict the use of your personal information, object to certain processing, and complain to a data protection authority.

1. What information do we collect?

Personal information you disclose to us

In short: We collect personal information that you provide to us.

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you express an interest in obtaining information about us or our services, request a quote, complete a contact form, or otherwise contact us.

Personal information provided by you may include, for example:

  • names;
  • phone numbers;
  • email addresses;
  • postal or mailing addresses;
  • company name and job role;
  • shipment and logistics details (such as origin, destination, approximate contents and timing) where relevant;
  • communication preferences and any other information you include in messages to us.

All personal information that you provide to us must be true, complete, and accurate, and you should notify us of any changes to such personal information.

Sensitive information

We do not intentionally collect or request special categories of data (for example, health data, religious beliefs, or information about sexual orientation). If such information is provided to us, we will aim to delete it where reasonably possible.

Information automatically collected

In short: Some information — such as your IP address and browser/device characteristics — is collected automatically when you visit our website.

We automatically collect certain technical information when you visit, use, or navigate the website. This information does not reveal your specific identity (such as your name or contact details) but may include:

  • IP address and general location (for example, country or city);
  • browser type and version, device type and operating system;
  • pages viewed, links clicked, and the date and time of your visits;
  • referring URLs and other usage data necessary to maintain security and improve our services.

Like many businesses, we may also use cookies and similar tracking technologies for analytics and to remember your preferences. See the section on cookies below for more detail.

2. How do we process your information?

In short: We process your information to provide, improve, and administer our services, communicate with you, for security and fraud prevention, and to comply with law. We may also process your information for other purposes with your consent.

We may process your personal information for, for example, the following purposes:

  • To deliver and manage services. To provide quotes, arrange and manage logistics services, and perform our contractual obligations.
  • To respond to enquiries and provide support. To answer questions, handle complaints, and provide customer service.
  • To operate, secure, and improve our website and services. To monitor usage, prevent abuse, troubleshoot issues, and analyse performance.
  • To comply with legal obligations. To meet tax, accounting, transport, customs, and other legal or regulatory requirements.
  • For marketing, where permitted. To send you updates or information about our services in accordance with your preferences and applicable law. You can opt out at any time.

3. What legal bases do we rely on?

In short: We only process your personal information when we believe it is necessary and we have a valid legal reason under applicable law.

Under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and Spanish law (LOPDGDD), we may rely on the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract: when processing is necessary to provide our services or to take steps at your request before entering into a contract.
  • Legal obligation: when processing is necessary to comply with laws (for example, tax and accounting rules, transport regulations, customs requirements).
  • Legitimate interests: when processing is necessary for our legitimate business interests (for example, to operate and improve our services, maintain security, or prevent fraud) and your interests and fundamental rights do not override those interests.
  • Consent: when we ask for your consent for specific purposes (for example, certain marketing or non-essential cookies). You may withdraw your consent at any time.

4. When and with whom do we share your information?

We may share your personal information with the following categories of recipients, only where necessary and subject to appropriate safeguards:

  • Service providers (processors) such as IT hosting providers, email services, CRM platforms, and other tools that help us operate our business. They process data only on our instructions and under confidentiality obligations.
  • Logistics and business partners such as carriers, agents, customs brokers, and other partners who need the information to perform the services you have requested.
  • Authorities or other third parties where required by law, to protect our rights, or to comply with legal proceedings.

We do not sell your personal information.

5. Do we use cookies and similar technologies?

We may use cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website to enable core functionality, analyse traffic and usage, and remember your preferences.

Where required by law, we will ask for your consent before using non-essential cookies. You can manage cookies through your browser settings and, where available, through cookie banners or preference centres on our website.

6. How long do we keep your information?

We keep your personal information only for as long as necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Notice, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law.

  • Contract and shipment data: retained for the duration of the relationship and for additional periods required by tax, accounting, or transport laws.
  • Communication data (such as emails and contact forms): retained for a reasonable period to manage enquiries and to document communications.
  • Marketing data: retained until you opt out or we no longer use it for the relevant purpose.

When data is no longer needed, we will delete it securely or anonymise it.

7. How do we keep your information safe?

We use appropriate technical and organisational measures designed to protect your personal information against unauthorised access, loss, alteration, or disclosure.

However, no electronic transmission over the internet or information storage technology can be guaranteed to be 100% secure, so we cannot promise that hackers or other unauthorised third parties will never be able to defeat our security measures.

8. Do we collect information from minors?

Our services are not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 16 years of age. If we learn that we have collected personal information from a child, we will take reasonable steps to delete it.

10. Controls for Do-Not-Track features

Most web browsers and some mobile operating systems include a Do-Not-Track ("DNT") feature or setting that you can activate to signal your privacy preference not to have data about your online browsing activities monitored and collected.

At this time, no uniform technology standard for recognising and implementing DNT signals has been finalised. Therefore, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals. Where required by applicable law, we will honour applicable browser-based opt-out signals such as Global Privacy Control (GPC) when implemented in your browser.

  • opt out of certain processing, such as targeted advertising or profiling, where applicable.

You can exercise these rights by using the data subject access request link above or by contacting us using the details in the "How can you contact us?" section.
